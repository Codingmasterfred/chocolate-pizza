To make the fig-swirl, start by melting butter in a saucepan over medium heat. Add brown sugar to the pan and stir until it dissolves completely. Next, halve the figs and add them to the saucepan along with water and lemon juice. Cook the mixture over medium heat, stirring frequently until it becomes a chunky-jammy consistency. Finally, add salt to the mixture and stir it a couple of times before setting it aside to cool completely. Once cooled, the fig-swirl is ready to be served and enjoyed.


To make the ice cream, start by combining milk and granulated sugar in a small pot over medium heat. Heat the mixture until the sugar is completely dissolved and the milk is just barely lukewarm. Next, whisk in the egg yolks and mix well. After that, set the mixture in the fridge and wait until the fig mixture is cooled. Once the fig mixture is cooled, combine it with the milk mixture and freeze it in an ice cream maker according to the manufacturer's instructions. Finally, transfer the ice cream to a container and freeze it for at least 2 hours or until it is firm enough to scoop.


To use an ice cream machine for making fig and mascarpone ice cream, start by pouring the liquid mixture into the frozen basin of the machine. Process the mixture according to the manufacturer's instructions, which usually involves spinning and thickening for about 20 minutes. After that, add the mascarpone, fig jam mixture, and the nuts to the mixture and continue processing for an additional 10 minutes or so. Once the mixture is semi-frozen, pour it into a Pyrex dish or glass Tupperware and let it chill in the freezer for at least two hours before serving. This will allow the ice cream to set properly and be ready to enjoy.

  • 11/2 cups milk
  • 1/2 cup mascarpone
  • 1/2 tsp pink salt
  • 1lb Black Mission Figs
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar
  • 2-4 tbsp water
  • 11/2 cups heavy cream
  • 4/3 granulated sugar & 2egg yolks
  • 1lemon, juiced
  • 2 tbsp butter
  • 1cup honey roasted pecans,roughly chopped
